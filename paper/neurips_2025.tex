\begin{abstract}
Comparing Large Language Model (LLM) variants---quantized checkpoints, fine-tuned snapshots, LoRA adaptations---faces a diagnostic gap: benchmarks report only an overall accuracy score that flags whether a variant has degraded but cannot say \emph{why}, and existing representational similarity metrics (e.g., CKA) offer no formal link to downstream performance, reducing all geometric mismatch to a single opaque number.
We propose \textbf{PRISM} (Proxy Risk Inference via Structural Mapping), a framework that upper-bounds the cross-entropy risk gap between a target model and its proxy by reducing it to three independently measurable axes derived from an \emph{exact closed-form decomposition} of the Procrustes feature residual---a scale mismatch $\Delta\rho$ and a shape mismatch $1-\Omega$---together with a covariance-weighted head-discrepancy term, each computable from a single teacher-forced forward pass per model.
We instantiate the framework for post-training quantization---reducing to a pure feature-geometry error under GPTQ and BitsAndBytes (which keep \texttt{lm\_head} intact) and retaining a quantified head correction under GGUF k-quant tiers that also quantize the output embedding---and for LoRA fine-tuning, where it isolates catastrophic forgetting in the backbone geometry; because its trace form $\Omega$ is differentiable, the same quantity lifts into a training-time \emph{shape regularizer} that suppresses downstream forgetting under fine-tuning.
Across four 8B-scale LLM lineages (Llama, Qwen, Ministral, DeepSeek) for quantization and two (Llama, Qwen) for LoRA forgetting, evaluated over five benchmarks, the bound ranks variant-level degradation with strong Spearman correlation, and its three axes localize three distinct empirical regimes---shape distortion at Q2/Q3 PTQ across all four families, output-embedding head divergence at Qwen3 Q6\_K/Q8\_0 with quantized \texttt{lm\_head}, and a separable scale axis evidenced by the BBQ-vs-TruthfulQA LoRA-drift contrast---turning a single-number similarity into a mechanism-level diagnostic that doubles, via $\Omega$, as a bound-motivated training-time regularizer.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

\paragraph{The diagnostic gap in the post-training LLM lifecycle.}
As large language models have moved from research prototypes to production deployments, a new engineering bottleneck has emerged downstream of pre-training.
A single base checkpoint now spawns a range of post-training variants---quantized at multiple bit-widths (GPTQ~\cite{frantar2022gptq}, GGUF, BitsAndBytes~\cite{dettmers2022gpt3}), adapted via LoRA~\cite{hu2022lora} for different downstream tasks, and distilled under different deployment configurations---each of which must be independently compared and iterated on before a deployed model is selected~\cite{grattafiori2024llama, qwen2025qwen3}.
Across these variants, the only signal evaluation routinely provides is an \emph{overall accuracy score} per benchmark---a single number that flags whether a variant has degraded but says nothing about \emph{why}, or which compression knob to turn next---and at this multiplicity, an un-diagnosable accuracy drop forces practitioners into blind trial-and-error rather than targeted correction.
An accuracy drop could equally trace to activation-magnitude collapse from outlier channels, to structural distortion of the feature manifold, or to quantization of the output embedding---three mechanisms each requiring a different remediation.

\paragraph{Similarity scores without risk, without mechanism.}
The natural shortcut is to borrow a representational similarity metric and declare two models ``close enough.''
A decade of internal-representation analysis has produced exactly this---SVCCA~\cite{raghu2017svcca}, linear and kernel CKA~\cite{kornblith2019similarity}, and a broader family of Frobenius-norm statistics---each reducing two activation matrices to a single alignment score.
Yet these scores do not pin down downstream behavior: a quantized model can read as highly similar and still lose accuracy, while a fine-tuned model can read as drifted and still retain task performance~\cite{ding2021grounding, davari2023reliability}, and the recent survey of~\cite{klabunde2023similarity} marks the link between representational and functional similarity as an open problem.
And even where they do correlate, a single-number similarity is no less opaque than the accuracy verdict above---it flags drift without localizing it to an axis that diagnosis could act on.
What is missing is a structural insight that promotes a geometric quantity to a functional risk bound \emph{while preserving the causal decomposition that diagnosis requires}.

\paragraph{What makes LLM geometry analytically tractable.}
What makes LLM geometry tractable for our purposes is a structural fact about Transformer architectures that prior similarity work has not exploited.
Every current Transformer language model factors cleanly into a deep non-linear backbone followed by a single linear prediction head (the \texttt{lm\_head}) that projects features onto the vocabulary; there is no additional non-linear classifier between features and logits.
The Linear Representation Hypothesis~\cite{moschella2022relative, park2023linear} further provides that models within a common lineage---a base and its quantized or fine-tuned descendants---learn feature manifolds related by a near-isometric linear map.
These two facts together make the geometry formally tractable: the feature-alignment residual under an Orthogonal Procrustes problem can be lifted \emph{exactly} into an upper bound on the cross-entropy risk gap---a single bound that, computed from one teacher-forced forward pass per model, uniformly covers point-wise classification, language modeling, short-horizon QA, and multi-step reasoning, with no ad-hoc constants and no black-box terms.
It is precisely because LLMs cleanly satisfy these structural conditions---a frozen linear head under standard LoRA, dominant outlier-channel scale collapse under PTQ~\cite{dettmers2022gpt3, xiao2023smoothquant}, and a shared cross-entropy scoring rule across these benchmark families---that we instantiate the framework exclusively on them.

\paragraph{PRISM: a bound with three actionable axes.}
We propose \textbf{PRISM} (Proxy Risk Inference via Structural Mapping), a framework that turns this structural insight into a bound-certified, mechanism-level diagnostic for LLM variants (Fig.~\ref{fig:prism_concept}).
Starting from the Procrustes alignment residual, we prove an \emph{exact} decomposition of the feature error into two orthogonal quantities: a \emph{scale mismatch} $\Delta\rho$ that isolates activation-magnitude collapse, and a \emph{shape mismatch} $1-\Omega$ that isolates distortion of the feature manifold, with $\Omega$ a nuclear-norm similarity strictly tighter than Frobenius-based surrogates---which themselves dominate standard CKA (Appendix~\ref{app:tightness}).
A third term---a head discrepancy, weighted by where the data actually lives---closes the bound.
Because each of these three quantities is individually measurable from a single forward pass, a PRISM failure is not merely a flag but a \emph{direction}: a scale-dominated failure points to outlier-channel clipping and argues for per-channel scaling~\cite{xiao2023smoothquant}; a shape-dominated failure points to the compression's Hessian approximation and argues for second-order methods such as GPTQ; a head-dominated failure localizes the loss to quantized output embeddings and argues for keeping the head in FP16.
Scale and shape are measurably distinct channels: in our LoRA experiments (Sec.~\ref{subsec:forget_exp}), BBQ fine-tuning drives drift along both axes while TruthfulQA fine-tuning drives primarily shape---a separation the decomposition captures and a unified similarity would collapse.
In our primary LLM regimes the optimal alignment is near-identity, so all experiments report a differentiable trace-form surrogate of $\Omega$ (denoted $\Omega$) evaluated at $W = I$; this joint choice keeps the head term at its minimum---vanishing exactly under frozen heads---while making the quantity autograd-compatible for gradient-based use in training (Sec.~\ref{subsec:interpretation}).
Sec.~\ref{sec:experiments} validates these axes across four LLM lineages and five benchmarks, with each axis localizing a distinct documented failure mode.

\paragraph{From diagnostic to training signal.}
The same quantity that diagnoses a variant's drift can also prevent it.
Under standard LoRA fine-tuning the prediction head is frozen, the head term of the bound vanishes by construction, and the risk gap is governed entirely by backbone scale and shape divergence, differentiable in the current features via the trace form $\Omega$.
Adding a weighted penalty on $1-\Omega$ to the fine-tuning objective therefore elevates the bound from a post-hoc lens into an active geometric constraint against catastrophic forgetting during training.
Empirically, even a weak penalty weight ($\lambda{=}0.1$) lifts $\Omega$ on every downstream benchmark---e.g., ARC $0.82{\to}0.86$, MMLU $0.87{\to}0.90$, GSM8K $0.94{\to}0.97$ on Llama-3.1-8B fine-tuned on TruthfulQA; increasing $\lambda$ to $0.5$ strengthens downstream preservation monotonically (ARC reaches $0.93$, GSM8K $1.00$), giving a bound-motivated knob for forgetting suppression under fine-tuning.

\paragraph{Contributions.}
Our contributions are threefold.
\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{An exact scale--shape decomposition that turns geometric similarity into diagnostic coordinates.} We prove that the Orthogonal Procrustes residual on a pair of feature matrices decomposes \emph{exactly} into a scale-mismatch and a shape-mismatch arm, promoting them to independently measurable diagnostic coordinates. Combined with a covariance-weighted head-discrepancy term, and using a feature-side cross-entropy Lipschitz constant derived via a simplex polarization argument (Appendix~\ref{app:kfeat}), this lifts Procrustes alignment into a closed-form upper bound on the cross-entropy risk gap (Theorem~\ref{thm:unified_bound}) whose three axes---scale collapse, structural distortion, head divergence---each carry a distinct operational reading that a single-number similarity cannot provide.

    \item \textbf{A unified framework across the LLM post-training lifecycle, usable both diagnostically and as a training objective.} The bound reduces to a pure feature-geometry error under GPTQ and BitsAndBytes (which keep the \texttt{lm\_head} intact), and retains a quantified head correction under GGUF k-quant tiers that also quantize the output embedding; under LoRA with a frozen head it isolates catastrophic forgetting in backbone divergence. Because its trace form $\Omega$ is differentiable, the same quantity doubles as a shape regularizer that actively suppresses forgetting during fine-tuning (Sec.~\ref{subsec:shape_reg}).

    \item \textbf{Rank-consistent empirical validation with three-axis failure-mode localization.} Across four 8B-scale LLM lineages (Llama, Qwen, Ministral, DeepSeek) for quantization and two (Llama, Qwen) for LoRA forgetting, evaluated over five benchmarks, the bound ranks variant-level degradation with mean Spearman $|r_s|{\approx}0.79$ across the 4{$\times$}5 quantization grid and preserves the same ranking structure under LoRA forgetting; its three axes localize distinct empirical regimes---shape distortion at Q2/Q3 across all four families, output-embedding head divergence at Qwen3 Q6\_K/Q8\_0 with quantized \texttt{lm\_head}, and a separable scale axis evidenced by the BBQ-vs-TruthfulQA LoRA-drift contrast. A mild shape-regularizer weight reproduces these gains during training, reducing forgetting on each of the five downstream benchmarks.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Related Work}

\paragraph{Representational Similarity and Procrustes Shape Metrics.}
Representational similarity statistics---SVCCA~\cite{raghu2017svcca}, CKA~\cite{kornblith2019similarity}, and the generalized shape metrics framework~\cite{williams2021generalized}---measure geometric resemblance between activation matrices, with the latter's squared Procrustes distance already carrying the nuclear norm $\|Z_T^\top Z_P\|_*$ that appears in our $\Omega_N$. The link from these summaries to downstream behavior remains an open problem~\cite{ding2021grounding, davari2023reliability, klabunde2023similarity}; a recent decodability bound~\cite{harvey2024what} reaches downstream via whitened kernels and freshly-optimized linear probes, a different object than the deployed prediction heads we evaluate. The Linear Representation Hypothesis~\cite{moschella2022relative, park2023linear} motivates restricting alignment to the orthogonal group, and the Platonic Representation Hypothesis~\cite{huh2024platonic} corroborates same-lineage shared geometry. Our formulation differs in retaining activation scale via $(\Delta\rho)^2$, modeling head mismatch through a covariance-weighted term, and lifting the Procrustes residual into a closed-form upper bound on cross-entropy risk evaluated on each model's deployed head.

\paragraph{Post-Training Quantization and Its Evaluation.}
Post-training quantization is the dominant deployment path for LLMs under memory constraints: GPTQ~\cite{frantar2022gptq} uses approximate second-order information for layer-wise reconstruction; GGUF and BitsAndBytes~\cite{dettmers2022gpt3} are simpler round-to-nearest schemes whose degradation tracks activation outliers~\cite{xiao2023smoothquant}. Existing cheap diagnostics---layer-wise reconstruction loss or weight quantization error---are indirect proxies that do not account for nonlinear error accumulation through the full network. PRISM contributes a complementary, generation-free geometric decomposition: scale collapse, structural distortion, and head divergence are each measured from a single forward pass, localizing which axis carries the degradation.

\paragraph{Catastrophic Forgetting under Parameter-Efficient Fine-Tuning.}
LoRA-based fine-tuning~\cite{hu2022lora} risks catastrophic forgetting of pre-training knowledge, even when the \texttt{lm\_head} stays frozen. Weight-space remedies---EWC~\cite{kirkpatrick2017overcoming} and replay-based methods---constrain parameter movement; a recent geometric analysis~\cite{steele2026subspace} empirically fits LoRA forgetting to \emph{gradient}-subspace angles. Our formulation uses \emph{hidden-state} geometry: feature-manifold drift ($\Omega$, $\Delta\rho$) relative to the frozen base bounds the downstream risk gap and is usable both as a post-hoc diagnostic and as a training-time penalty (Sec.~\ref{subsec:shape_reg}).

\paragraph{Efficient Evaluation and Performance Forecasting.}
Scaling laws~\cite{kaplan2020scaling, hoffmann2022training} predict aggregate loss but not variant-specific degradation; item-response-theory subsets~\cite{polo2024tinybenchmarks, perlitz2024efficient} preserve variant orderings without linking representational change to expected accuracy shift; weak-to-strong generalization~\cite{burns2023weak} targets label generation rather than risk estimation. PRISM is geometry-grounded: the bound is computed directly from representational geometry and decomposes each variant's degradation into three diagnostic axes.

\section{A Geometric Framework for Proxy Performance Estimation}
\label{sec:geometric_foundation}

We derive a closed-form upper bound on the cross-entropy risk gap $|\mathcal{R}_T - \mathcal{R}_P|$ between a target model and a proxy, grounded entirely in their feature geometry. We first state the setup, then prove an exact decomposition of the bound into three diagnostic axes (scale, shape, head), explain the operational form ($\Omega$ at $W{=}I$) used throughout our experiments, extend the bound to autoregressive generation, and finally lift it into a training-time regularizer.

\subsection{Setup}
\label{subsec:problem_setup}

We consider a \emph{Target model} $T$ (e.g., a full-precision base) and a \emph{Proxy model} $P$ (e.g., a quantized or fine-tuned variant) with shared hidden dimension $d$. Each model factors into a Transformer backbone $\phi_M: \mathcal{X} \to \mathbb{R}^d$ followed by a linear prediction head $H_M \in \mathbb{R}^{d \times V}$ (the \texttt{lm\_head} projecting onto a vocabulary of size $V$):
\begin{equation}
f_M(x) = \mathrm{softmax}\!\big(\phi_M(x) \cdot H_M\big), \qquad M \in \{T, P\}.
\end{equation}
The risk of $M$ under data distribution $\mathcal{D}$ is the cross-entropy expectation
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:ce_def}
\mathcal{R}_M \;=\; \mathbb{E}_{(x,y) \sim \mathcal{D}}\!\big[\ell\big(\phi_M(x) H_M,\, y\big)\big], \qquad \ell(v, y) = -v_y + \log \textstyle\sum_{j} e^{v_j}, \; v \in \mathbb{R}^V.
\end{equation}
Following the \emph{Linear Representation Hypothesis}~\cite{moschella2022relative, park2023linear}, we assume the two backbones lie in a common lineage: there exists an orthogonal $W \in \mathcal{O}(d)$ such that $\phi_T(x) \approx \phi_P(x)\, W$ for all $x$. On a calibration sample of size $n$, we collect features into $Z_T, Z_P \in \mathbb{R}^{n \times d}$, denote the columns of $H_M$ by $h_{M,1},\ldots,h_{M,V}$, and define the RMS feature scale $\rho_M = \|Z_M\|_F / \sqrt{n}$ and the empirical second-moment matrix $\Sigma_P = Z_P^\top Z_P / n$.


\subsection{The Unified Risk Bound}
\label{subsec:unified_bound}

To bound $|\mathcal{R}_T - \mathcal{R}_P|$ we introduce a hybrid risk $\mathcal{R}_{P \to T} := \mathbb{E}[\ell(\phi_P(x)\,W \cdot H_T, y)]$ that evaluates the target's head on the proxy's aligned features. The triangle inequality splits the gap into a \emph{feature error} $\delta := |\mathcal{R}_T - \mathcal{R}_{P\to T}|$ and a \emph{head error} $\gamma := |\mathcal{R}_{P\to T} - \mathcal{R}_P|$; each admits a closed-form bound whose full derivation is in Appendix~\ref{app:detailed_proofs}.

\paragraph{Feature error $\delta$.}
The cross-entropy loss is Lipschitz in the feature vector with constant $K_{\mathrm{feat}}$, derived via a simplex polarization argument in Appendix~\ref{app:kfeat}. The alignment residual $\|Z_T - Z_P W\|_F^2 / n$ admits an exact identity for every orthogonal $W$:

\begin{proposition}[Exact Scale--Shape Decomposition]
\label{prop:exact_decomposition}
For any $W \in \mathcal{O}(d)$,
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:exact_equality}
\frac{1}{n} \| Z_T - Z_P W \|_F^2
\;=\;
\underbrace{(\rho_T - \rho_P)^2}_{\text{\rm Scale Mismatch } (\Delta\rho)^2}
\;+\;
\underbrace{2 \rho_T \rho_P \big( 1 - \Omega_W \big)}_{\text{\rm Shape Mismatch}},
\end{equation}
where $\Omega_W$ is the \emph{trace Procrustes similarity}:
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:omega_def}
\Omega_W(Z_T, Z_P) \;:=\; \frac{\operatorname{Tr}(Z_T^\top Z_P\, W)}{\| Z_T \|_F\, \| Z_P \|_F} \;\in [-1,\, 1].
\end{equation}
\end{proposition}

Combined with the Lipschitz bound (Appendix~\ref{app:detailed_proofs}):
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:delta_bound}
\delta \;\le\; K_{\mathrm{feat}} \sqrt{(\rho_T - \rho_P)^2 + 2\rho_T \rho_P (1 - \Omega_W)}.
\end{equation}

\paragraph{Head error $\gamma$.}
Let $\Delta H := W H_T - H_P$ for the alignment $W$; then $\gamma \le K_{\mathrm{pred}}\,\|\Sigma_P^{1/2}\Delta H\|_F$ with $K_{\mathrm{pred}} \le \sqrt{2}$ (Appendix~\ref{app:head_bound}). The covariance weighting $\Sigma_P^{1/2}$ ensures that only head misalignment within the \emph{active subspace} of the data contributes.

\begin{theorem}[Unified Risk Bound]
\label{thm:unified_bound}
For any $W \in \mathcal{O}(d)$,
\begin{equation}
|\mathcal{R}_T - \mathcal{R}_P| \;\le\; \underbrace{K_{\mathrm{feat}} \sqrt{ (\rho_T - \rho_P)^2 + 2\rho_T \rho_P \big(1 - \Omega_W\big) }}_{\text{\rm Geometric Alignment Error}} \;+\; \underbrace{K_{\mathrm{pred}} \,\| \Sigma_P^{1/2} (W H_T - H_P) \|_F}_{\text{\rm Second-Moment-Weighted Head Error}}.
\end{equation}
\end{theorem}


\subsection{Three Diagnostic Axes}
\label{subsec:interpretation}

Theorem~\ref{thm:unified_bound} decomposes the risk gap along three \emph{independently measurable} axes, each attached to a distinct failure mode of the proxy.

\paragraph{Scale mismatch $\Delta\rho$.}
Divergence in activation magnitude directly amplifies the feature error. Aggressive bit-width reduction clips activation outliers~\cite{dettmers2022gpt3, xiao2023smoothquant}, shrinking $\rho_P < \rho_T$; the scale axis isolates this \emph{scale collapse}.

\paragraph{Shape mismatch $1-\Omega_W$.}
The shape term captures distortion of the feature manifold up to rotation. A drop in $\Omega_W$ signals that the relative arrangement of token representations has been corrupted---what we term \emph{structural distortion}.

\paragraph{Head divergence $\|\Sigma_P^{1/2}(W H_T - H_P)\|_F$.}
The head term quantifies how differently the two prediction heads interpret features, weighted by $\Sigma_P$ so that only directions along which the data actually lives can contribute.

\paragraph{PRISM accommodates a family of Procrustes similarities.}
The family $\{\Omega_W\}$ of Prop.~\ref{prop:exact_decomposition} includes the trace form $\Omega_{W=I}$ and the nuclear form $\Omega_N := \Omega_{W=W_N}$ (Appendix~\ref{app:tightness}); a related Frobenius similarity $\Omega_F$, tied to CKA, is discussed there as an external comparison. The main text commits to $\Omega := \Omega_{W=I}$ because the frozen-head regimes studied here---LoRA and FP16-head PTQ---keep $H_T = H_P$ in most configurations, so the head term simplifies at $W = I$ and leaves the risk gap clean to analyze; the $W = W_N$ counterpart is reported as a Sec.~\ref{subsec:ablation} ablation.


\subsection{Extension to Autoregressive Generation}
\label{subsec:ar_extension}

The bound applies unchanged to sequence-level generation. For a sequence $(c, y)$ with context $c$ and target continuation $y = (y_1, \ldots, y_{|y|})$, the autoregressive risk is
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:ar_risk}
\mathcal{R}_M^{\mathrm{AR}} \;=\; \mathbb{E}_{(c, y) \sim \mathcal{D}} \!\left[ \frac{1}{|y|} \sum_{\tau=1}^{|y|} \ell\!\big(\phi_M(c, y_{<\tau}) \cdot H_M,\; y_\tau\big) \right].
\end{equation}
Under teacher forcing, all token-level features $\phi_M(c, y_{<\tau})$ are extracted in a single forward pass and collected into a matrix $Z_M^{\mathrm{AR}} \in \mathbb{R}^{N \times d}$ (with $N$ the total number of target tokens across the calibration set); Theorem~\ref{thm:unified_bound} then applies directly to $(Z_T^{\mathrm{AR}}, Z_P^{\mathrm{AR}})$, unifying point-wise classification, language modeling, short-horizon QA, and multi-step reasoning under a single bound. The full corollary is in Appendix~\ref{app:ar_extension}.


\subsection{From Diagnostic to Training: Shape Regularization}
\label{subsec:shape_reg}

LoRA fine-tuning drives the backbone shape away from its base: $\Omega(Z_0, Z_t)$ drops as training proceeds, and---because the head is frozen---the full risk gap between base $\theta_0$ and checkpoint $\theta_t$ reduces to backbone drift alone,
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:lora_bound}
|\mathcal{R}_0 - \mathcal{R}_t| \;\le\; K_{\mathrm{feat}}\sqrt{(\rho_0-\rho_t)^2 + 2\rho_0\rho_t(1-\Omega)}.
\end{equation}
Pinning shape should therefore stabilize target-task learning and suppress catastrophic forgetting on downstream tasks at once. Since $1 - \Omega$ is differentiable in $Z_t$, we augment the fine-tuning objective with a shape-preserving penalty:
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:shape_reg}
L_{\mathrm{total}} \;=\; \underbrace{L_{\mathrm{CE}}\bigl(\theta_t;\,\mathcal{D}_{\mathrm{FT}}\bigr)}_{\text{task loss}} \;+\; \lambda\,\bigl(1 - \Omega\!\bigl(Z_0^{\text{ref}},\, Z_t^{\text{ref}}\bigr)\bigr),
\end{equation}
where $Z_0^{\text{ref}}, Z_t^{\text{ref}}$ are base and current feature matrices on a fixed reference set (training-side schedule and overhead in Sec.~\ref{subsec:exp_setup}); empirical effect is validated in Sec.~\ref{subsec:shape_reg_exp}.


\section{Applications Across the LLM Post-Training Lifecycle}
\label{sec:applications}

The Unified Risk Bound (Theorem~\ref{thm:unified_bound}) applies to any two models within a common lineage. We instantiate it below in two lifecycle settings---post-training quantization and LoRA fine-tuning with a frozen head---both of which satisfy the near-identity alignment condition ($W_N \approx I$) discussed in Sec.~\ref{subsec:interpretation}, so the operational trace form $\Omega$ applies in each; the two settings differ in which term of the bound dominates the risk gap.

\subsection{Quantization Quality Estimation}
\label{subsec:quantization}

We designate the full-precision model as Target $T$ and the quantized variant as Proxy $P$; Theorem~\ref{thm:unified_bound} applies directly. Two PTQ-specific conditions determine which term dominates the risk gap. First, standard PTQ introduces element-wise numerical noise that preserves the coordinate basis ($W_N \approx I$), so the feature error reduces to scale collapse $(\Delta\rho)^2$---capturing the activation clipping induced by outlier channels~\cite{dettmers2022gpt3, xiao2023smoothquant}---and structural distortion $1 - \Omega$---capturing corruption of the relational geometry between token representations. Second, GPTQ and most BitsAndBytes configurations retain the \texttt{lm\_head} in FP16 and the head-divergence term $\|\Sigma_P^{1/2}(W H_T - H_P)\|_F$ vanishes, but several GGUF k-quant tiers (e.g., \texttt{Q2\_K}, \texttt{Q3\_K\_M}) also quantize the output embedding and contribute a non-negligible head discrepancy term. All three terms are generation-free and computable from a single forward pass, letting practitioners rank quantization configurations from feature statistics without decoding.


\subsection{Geometric Monitoring of Catastrophic Forgetting}
\label{subsec:forgetting}

We designate the frozen base model as $T = \theta_0$ and the fine-tuned checkpoint at step $t$ as $P = \theta_t$, and evaluate Theorem~\ref{thm:unified_bound} on tasks \emph{other than} the one being fine-tuned on---this downstream risk is the empirical signature of catastrophic forgetting. Under standard LoRA~\cite{hu2022lora}, low-rank updates are confined to attention and MLP projections while the \texttt{lm\_head} stays frozen ($H_t = H_0$), so the head-divergence term vanishes and forgetting is governed \emph{entirely} by backbone geometric drift $(\Delta\rho, 1-\Omega)$---the very quantity that the shape regularizer of Sec.~\ref{subsec:shape_reg} minimizes during training. The result is a label-free diagnostic that decouples backbone-geometry drift from head misalignment.

\section{Experiments}
\label{sec:experiments}

\subsection{Experimental Setup}
\label{subsec:exp_setup}

\paragraph{Models.}
Our experiments span two regimes with different model coverage. For \textbf{quantization} (Sec.~\ref{subsec:quant_exp}) we evaluate four 8B open-weight models spanning the major lineages: Llama-3.1-8B~\cite{grattafiori2024llama}, Ministral-3-8B, Qwen3-8B~\cite{qwen2025qwen3}, and DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Llama-8B, giving cross-family evidence that the bound is not a per-lineage artifact; we additionally replicate the same quantization grid on their instruction-tuned counterparts (Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct, Ministral-3-8B-Instruct, Qwen3-8B-Instruct) in Appendix~\ref{app:per_model_tables} to verify that the bound tracks degradation on instruction-tuned checkpoints as well as on base models. For \textbf{fine-tuning forgetting and shape regularization} (Secs.~\ref{subsec:forget_exp}--\ref{subsec:shape_reg_exp}) we restrict the main text to two models---Llama-3.1-8B and Qwen3-8B---with Qwen results in Appendix~\ref{app:qwen_forgetting}. This pair differs in every major pre-training variable (corpus, depth, vocabulary, tokenizer family), providing cross-lineage evidence for the bound's lineage-invariance; the variable-by-variable breakdown is in Appendix~\ref{app:qwen_forgetting}. We exclude DeepSeek-R1-Distill (reasoning-distilled, atypical LoRA dynamics) and Ministral (multimodal architecture with nested language-model backbone) because their fine-tuning behaviour deviates from standard practice.

\paragraph{Quantization Protocols.}
We test three PTQ families across bit-widths 2--8: \textbf{GGUF} (round-to-nearest, \texttt{Q2\_K} through \texttt{Q8\_0}), \textbf{GPTQ}~\cite{frantar2022gptq} (4-bit, second-order reconstruction), and \textbf{BitsAndBytes}~\cite{dettmers2022gpt3} (INT8, NF4, FP4). This covers the spectrum from basic rounding to calibration-based compensation.

\paragraph{Fine-Tuning Tasks.}
LoRA fine-tuning is performed on two complementary tasks chosen to elicit distinct forgetting profiles. \textbf{TruthfulQA} is a truthfulness-oriented QA set designed to trigger plausible-but-wrong generations, stressing safety-critical factual grounding. \textbf{BBQ} (Bias Benchmark for QA) measures bias-sensitive reasoning through ambiguous social-context questions. The two are complementary---TruthfulQA perturbs factual behaviour, BBQ perturbs reasoning under social bias---so downstream forgetting on general-knowledge benchmarks (ARC, MMLU, SQuAD, TriviaQA, GSM8K) provides a non-trivial stress test of whether PRISM tracks drift across task families, not just within a single domain.

\paragraph{Benchmarks.}
Following the autoregressive risk formulation (Eq.~\ref{eq:ar_risk}), we organize benchmarks by the partition between context $c$ and target $y$:
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Multiple-Choice (MC)} --- MMLU, ARC: $c$ is the full prompt including all choices, $y$ is the answer label token (a single token per question). We extract the final prompt token's feature and compare logit scores over the candidate labels, reducing the task to single-token classification.
    \item \textbf{Language Modeling (LM)} --- WikiText, FineWeb-Edu: $c$ is empty or a rolling prefix, $y$ is a contiguous text chunk. Risk is the standard sequence-level cross-entropy.
    \item \textbf{Short-Horizon Generation (QA)} --- TriviaQA, SQuAD: $c$ is the question prompt, $y$ is the answer span (1--5 tokens). Loss is evaluated strictly over answer tokens.
    \item \textbf{Multi-Step Reasoning} --- GSM8K: $c$ is the math word problem, $y$ is the gold chain-of-thought solution culminating in the numeric answer (typically tens to a hundred tokens under teacher forcing). Loss is evaluated over the full reasoning span.
\end{itemize}
The main quantitative results of Secs.~\ref{subsec:quant_exp}--\ref{subsec:forget_exp} focus on the five MC, QA, and reasoning benchmarks (ARC, MMLU, SQuAD, TriviaQA, GSM8K); the two language-modeling benchmarks WikiText and FineWeb-Edu follow the same pattern and are reported in Appendix~\ref{app:per_model_tables}.

\paragraph{Calibration set size.}
All PRISM quantities and empirical risk gaps are computed on fixed held-out subsets held constant across all variants of a given base model: $N{=}512$ items per benchmark for the quantization regime, and $N{=}256$ items per downstream benchmark for the LoRA forgetting regime (the smaller fine-tuning tasks TruthfulQA and BBQ are evaluated on their full test sets). Two observations justify this protocol.
(i)~\textbf{Fixed-subset design neutralizes subset-selection noise.} Because the \emph{same} $N$-item subset is evaluated by every variant of a model, subset-selection variance enters every $|\Delta\mathcal{R}|$ and every bound estimate equally and therefore does not affect the variant-to-variant \emph{ranking}---the quantity we ultimately report via Spearman correlation.
(ii)~\textbf{Signal dominates sampling noise.} The orderings we rank distinguish variants whose true $|\Delta\mathcal{R}|$ values span one to two orders of magnitude (e.g., Q2\_K vs.\ Q8\_0, Table~\ref{tab:llama_decomposition_main}), a regime in which rank preservation is robust at moderate $N$.

\paragraph{Shape-Regularization Hyperparameters.}
For Sec.~\ref{subsec:shape_reg_exp} we sweep $\lambda \in \{0.0,\, 0.1,\, 0.5\}$, where $\lambda{=}0$ reproduces unregularized LoRA. The reference set used to compute $\Omega$ is a fixed held-out batch of $32$ sequences drawn from the base model's pre-training distribution (not from $\mathcal{D}_{\mathrm{FT}}$, so that the penalty and task loss do not directly interfere). $Z_t^{\text{ref}}$ is recomputed every $k{=}8$ micro-steps; checkpoints are saved every 50 training steps, and all analysis figures/tables align to step $300$.

\paragraph{Teacher-Forced Scoring Protocol.}
All risks in this paper are computed in the teacher-forced regime: the prompt $c$ and the target tokens $y$ are concatenated and passed through the model once, and the per-token loss is read off at the target positions. This matches the scoring rules of every benchmark above by construction (MC by gold-label likelihood, perplexity by definition, short-answer QA over the gold span, and reasoning over the gold chain-of-thought span), and offers three concrete benefits: a single parallel forward pass replaces token-by-token conditional decoding (sidestepping KV-cache growth and sampling overhead); teacher-forced cross-entropy is also the LoRA fine-tuning objective, so calibration and training loss live on the same scale, unlike decoding-based metrics such as BLEU or pass@$k$; and removing the sampling stage eliminates temperature, top-$k$, and nucleus randomness, producing deterministic per-sample residuals. We do not claim teacher-forced cross-entropy substitutes for generation-based evaluation in open-ended tasks; PRISM is a variant-ranking signal complementary to full generative evaluation.

\input{tables/quantization/table_llama_main}

Figure~\ref{fig:quant_grid_bound} summarizes the main quantization result: the PRISM bound $\mathcal{B}$ tracks $|\Delta\mathcal{R}|$ with strong rank correlation across four lineages and five benchmarks. Two qualitative patterns hold consistently. First, \emph{bit-width drives the bound monotonically}: Q8 and Q6 GGUF variants sit in the low-$\mathcal{B}$, low-$|\Delta\mathcal{R}|$ corner, while Q2 configurations move into the upper-right region where shape distortion $(1-\Omega)$ dominates the feature error. Second, \emph{family ordering is preserved across benchmarks}: for a given bit-width, BitsAndBytes NF4/FP4 and GGUF Q3 cluster together, while GPTQ-4bit (evaluated on Llama and DeepSeek) is typically positioned one notch lower in $\mathcal{B}$, reflecting its second-order Hessian compensation. Table~\ref{tab:llama_decomposition_main} reports the per-variant decomposition for Llama-3.1-8B on MMLU and TriviaQA (remaining benchmarks and the other three lineages are in Appendix~\ref{app:per_model_tables}): at the extreme Q2\_K TriviaQA case, $\rho_P$ exceeds $\rho_T$ only mildly ($\Delta\rho\approx 1.3$), but $\Omega$ drops to $0.76$, driving the shape-mismatch arm of the bound to dominate. This separation between scale and shape channels, made explicit by our decomposition, is not visible in a single-number similarity metric. A component-wise ablation of the bound's ranking contributions is in Sec.~\ref{subsec:ablation} (Table~\ref{tab:baseline_combined}); the full feature-only $4{\times}5$ scatter, per-model decomposition tables, and the parallel WikiText/FineWeb-Edu language-modeling results (which exhibit the same bit-width-monotone pattern) are in Appendices~\ref{app:feature_only}--\ref{app:quant_tables}.

\paragraph{Which axis fails where.}
The decomposition isolates three qualitatively distinct PTQ failure modes in our data, each dominated by a different axis of Theorem~\ref{thm:unified_bound}. \emph{Shape distortion} dominates the majority of aggressive quantization configurations: at Q2 and Q3 across all four families, the shape term $2\rho_T\rho_P(1{-}\Omega)$ outweighs the scale term $(\Delta\rho)^2$ by two to three orders of magnitude (e.g., Llama-Q2\_K on MMLU: $24$ vs.~$\sim$$9{,}000$; Ministral-Q2\_K on MMLU: $\sim$$9$ vs.~$\sim$$11{,}000$)---consistent with low-bit PTQ corrupting the relational structure of the feature manifold rather than its global scale. \emph{Scale} drift is measurable across our PTQ grid but does not cleanly dominate any single variant; the most scale-visible PTQ case is Qwen3-Base Q2\_K on GSM8K (Table~\ref{tab:qwen_decomposition_all} in Appendix~\ref{app:per_model_tables}), where $\rho_P$ inflates from $267$ to $313$ alongside shape drift. \emph{Head divergence} isolates a third regime entirely: at Qwen3-Base Q6\_K on MMLU (Table~\ref{tab:qwen_decomposition_all} in Appendix~\ref{app:per_model_tables}), backbone scale and shape are essentially perfect ($(\Delta\rho)^2{=}0.56$, $\Omega{=}1.0000$) so $\delta{=}0.75$, yet the quantized output embedding alone contributes $\gamma{=}60.16$---making $\gamma$ essentially the entire bound (Q8\_0 shows the same pattern: $\delta{=}0.15$, $\gamma{=}19.0$). Under BnB INT8 the same Qwen3-Base keeps $\gamma{\equiv}0$ by construction, leaving $\delta{=}13.2$ as the sole error source---a qualitatively different decomposition driven by which protocol quantizes the \texttt{lm\_head}. Each dominant axis suggests a different remediation---per-channel outlier smoothing~\cite{xiao2023smoothquant} for scale collapse, Hessian-aware reconstruction for shape distortion, and FP16-head retention for head divergence---turning the bound from a scalar score into a mechanism-level diagnostic.

Figure~\ref{fig:forget_grid_llama} establishes the forgetting regime's key claim: as LoRA fine-tuning proceeds on a task (TruthfulQA or BBQ), the PRISM bound computed on \emph{downstream} benchmarks rises in lockstep with the empirical cross-entropy drift. Because the head is frozen, all of this signal originates in the backbone---the drift of $\Omega(Z_0, Z_t)$ away from $1$ and of $\rho_t$ away from $\rho_0$. The decomposition makes a structural observation visible that a unified distance would collapse: \emph{different source tasks induce qualitatively different drift geometries}. TruthfulQA fine-tuning drives large shape drift with modest scale change, while BBQ fine-tuning moves both axes; the decomposition into $(\Delta\rho, 1{-}\Omega)$ in Eq.~(\ref{eq:lora_bound}) therefore exposes the two arms as non-redundant channels and supplies the cleanest empirical signature of scale-axis separability in our experiments. A second pattern is that \emph{the correlation holds benchmark-by-benchmark}, not only in aggregate: the bound tracks drift on each of the five downstream benchmarks (including GSM8K, whose distribution differs substantially from the fine-tuning data), evidence that backbone geometry carries the forgetting signal across distinct evaluation families. The Qwen3-8B replication (Appendix~\ref{app:qwen_forgetting}) reproduces both patterns.

Figure~\ref{fig:shape_reg_combined_llama} and Table~\ref{tab:trace_norm_llama_truthfulqa} together answer the central empirical question raised by Sec.~\ref{subsec:shape_reg}: does minimizing $(1-\Omega)$ during fine-tuning actually suppress forgetting? For Llama-3.1-8B fine-tuned on TruthfulQA, Table~\ref{tab:trace_norm_llama_truthfulqa} gives a concrete decomposition at step $300$: as $\lambda$ rises from $0.0$ to $0.5$, $\Omega$ on downstream benchmarks increases monotonically (e.g., ARC $0.82\!\to\!0.86\!\to\!0.93$, MMLU $0.87\!\to\!0.90\!\to\!0.95$, GSM8K $0.94\!\to\!0.97\!\to\!1.00$), and the downstream $|\Delta\mathcal{R}|$ drops on every benchmark. Third, even the mildest setting $\lambda{=}0.1$ already eliminates a substantial fraction of forgetting, suggesting that a weak geometric prior suffices to stabilize the backbone. \subsection{Ablation: Component-Wise Contribution to Variant Ranking}
\label{subsec:ablation}

\input{tables/quantization/baseline_combined}

Table~\ref{tab:baseline_combined} isolates the marginal ranking contribution of each component of the PRISM bound by progressively adding them. Under the operational $W{=}I$ form (top block), the shape-only similarity $\Omega$ already achieves mean $|r_s|{=}0.77$, confirming that the relational geometry of token features alone carries most of the variant-ranking signal. Adding the scale term to form the feature alignment error $\delta$ lifts the mean by $+0.054$ to $0.82$ and wins the plurality of cells, making scale collapse the single largest per-component contributor. Adding the covariance-weighted head term to form the full bound $\mathcal{B}$ brings the aggregate mean slightly down to $0.79$: because our suite mixes protocols that quantize the output embedding ($\gamma{>}0$ for GGUF k-quant tiers) with protocols that keep it in FP16 ($\gamma{\equiv}0$ for GPTQ and BnB), the $\gamma$ term injects non-monotone variance across the pooled scatter. Two observations keep $\mathcal{B}$ the right \emph{default} metric. First, it is the certified upper bound of Theorem~\ref{thm:unified_bound} and is strictly required whenever $\gamma{>}0$; $\delta$ alone is not a valid bound in that regime. Second, $\mathcal{B}$ has the smallest cross-lineage spread (per-lineage std $\approx 0.04$ vs.\ $0.05$--$0.06$ for $\Omega$ and $\delta$), so its ranking performance is the most robust across the four model families tested.

Under the Procrustes-optimal alignment $W{=}W_N$ (bottom block), the cumulative pattern reverses cleanly: $\Omega_N \to \delta_N \to \mathcal{B}_N$ all increase monotonically, and the full bound $\mathcal{B}_N$ attains the strongest aggregate ranking ($|r_s|{=}0.857$, $17/20$ cells)---confirming that the head term is genuinely beneficial \emph{when the alignment can absorb the non-identity rotations} between $H_T$ and $H_P$, and that the non-monotonicity observed under $W{=}I$ traces to the alignment, not to the bound design itself. The two blocks together validate Theorem~\ref{thm:unified_bound} at $W_N$ and quantify the operational trade-off at $W{=}I$: the trace specialization sacrifices $\sim$$0.07$ Spearman for autograd compatibility (no SVD per step), the head-term simplification under frozen \texttt{lm\_head}, and consistency with the regularizer of Sec.~\ref{subsec:shape_reg}. We continue to recommend $\mathcal{B}$ as the operational default and $\mathcal{B}_N$ as a post-hoc reference when the additional SVD cost is acceptable.

\section{Conclusion and Future Work}
\label{sec:conclusion}

We presented PRISM, a geometric framework that bounds the cross-entropy risk gap $|\mathcal{R}_T - \mathcal{R}_P|$ along three independently measurable axes---scale collapse, structural distortion, and head divergence---derived from an exact Procrustes decomposition of feature alignment (Theorem~\ref{thm:unified_bound}). Instantiated for post-training quantization and LoRA-forgetting monitoring, the bound ranks variants with strong Spearman correlation, localizes three distinct empirical regimes along its three axes---shape distortion at Q2/Q3 PTQ across all four families, output-embedding head divergence at Qwen3 Q6\_K/Q8\_0 with quantized \texttt{lm\_head}, and a separable scale axis evidenced by the BBQ-vs-TruthfulQA LoRA-drift contrast---and, via the differentiable trace form $\Omega$, yields a training-time shape regularizer that suppresses catastrophic forgetting on each downstream benchmark.

\paragraph{Limitations and Future Work.}
Three scope boundaries delimit the present work. First, the bound's tightness depends on the Linear Representation Hypothesis approximation; comparisons across models that violate near-isometric alignment (e.g., adversarially re-parameterized variants) fall outside our scope. Second, our experiments are confined to the 8B production scale---validation at smaller (3B) and larger (70B+) scales is left to future work. Third, the per-axis remediations (per-channel outlier smoothing, Hessian-aware reconstruction, FP16-head retention) are \emph{argued for} via the bound's structural decomposition; experimentally verifying that each remediation closes the corresponding axis is a natural next step we do not attempt here.

Beyond these boundaries, the bound supports diagnostic uses we leave to future validation---per-sample \emph{OOD detection} via scale and shape residuals, \emph{cross-scale hyperparameter transfer} bounded by $\Omega$ between small and large proxies, and \emph{label-free production monitoring} since $\Omega$ and $\Delta\rho$ depend only on feature matrices. Several substantive extensions remain:
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Dynamic Trajectory Alignment:} Our current formulation validates static (prefill) feature manifolds. Extending to multi-step generation trajectories would enable proxy estimation for long Chain-of-Thought reasoning and agentic workflows.
    \item \textbf{Cross-Lineage Proxy Scenarios:} The current framework targets same-lineage comparisons (a base and its post-training descendants). Extending the alignment step beyond orthogonal Procrustes would naturally support proxy scenarios that bridge different lineages---such as teacher--student distillation---generalizing PRISM to a broader class of model comparisons.
    \item \textbf{Cross-Modal Generalization:} The Linear Representation Hypothesis extends beyond text. Investigating PRISM's applicability to vision transformers and cross-modal models (e.g., CLIP) remains a natural next step.
\end{itemize}
