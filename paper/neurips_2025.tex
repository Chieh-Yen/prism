\begin{abstract}
Comparing Large Language Model (LLM) variants---quantized checkpoints, fine-tuned snapshots, LoRA adaptations---faces a diagnostic gap: aggregate benchmark accuracy flags degradation without explaining it, and existing representational similarity metrics (e.g., CKA) reduce geometric mismatch to a single aggregate score without linking to downstream performance.
We propose \textbf{PRISM} (Proxy Risk Inference via Structural Mapping), a framework that upper-bounds the cross-entropy risk gap between a target and proxy model by combining an \emph{exact closed-form decomposition} of the feature alignment residual (scale mismatch $\Delta\rho$, shape mismatch $1-\Omega$) with a covariance-weighted head discrepancy.
Across four 8B-scale LLM lineages and five benchmarks, the bound ranks variant degradation with strong Spearman correlation, and its three axes localize distinct empirical regimes under post-training quantization and LoRA fine-tuning, turning a scalar similarity into an axis-level diagnostic.
Because $\Omega$ is differentiable, the shape term doubles as a training-time regularizer against catastrophic forgetting---extending PRISM from a diagnostic tool into an active training signal.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

\paragraph{The diagnostic gap in the post-training LLM lifecycle.}
As large language models move from research prototypes to production deployments, a new engineering bottleneck has emerged downstream of pre-training.
A single base checkpoint now yields many post-training variants---quantized (GPTQ~\cite{frantar2022gptq}, GGUF, BitsAndBytes~\cite{dettmers2022gpt3}), LoRA-adapted~\cite{hu2022lora}, or distilled under different deployment configurations---each of which must be compared before a deployed model is selected~\cite{grattafiori2024llama, qwen2025qwen3}.
Evaluation gives only an aggregate accuracy score per benchmark, so a degraded variant is flagged but its cause is not: practitioners resort to blind trial-and-error rather than targeted correction.

\paragraph{Similarity scores without risk, without mechanism.}
A natural shortcut is to borrow a representational similarity metric: SVCCA~\cite{raghu2017svcca}, CKA~\cite{kornblith2019similarity}, and related Frobenius-norm metrics each reduce two activation matrices to a single alignment score.
These scores, however, do not pin down downstream behavior~\cite{ding2021grounding, davari2023reliability, klabunde2023similarity}, and a single number still flags drift without identifying an axis for diagnosis.
What is missing is a geometric quantity that lifts into a functional risk bound while keeping its causal decomposition intact.

\paragraph{What makes LLM geometry analytically tractable.}
Transformer LLMs factor cleanly into a non-linear backbone and a single linear prediction head (\texttt{lm\_head}), with no intermediate non-linear projection between features and vocabulary logits.
The Linear Representation Hypothesis~\cite{moschella2022relative, park2023linear} further asserts that same-lineage variants learn feature manifolds related by a near-isometric linear map.
These two structural facts let us lift the Procrustes feature-alignment residual into an \emph{exact} closed-form upper bound on the cross-entropy risk gap.

\paragraph{PRISM: a bound with three actionable axes.}
We propose \textbf{PRISM} (Proxy Risk Inference via Structural Mapping; Fig.~\ref{fig:prism_concept}), a principled, axis-level diagnostic for LLM variants.
An exact decomposition of the Procrustes residual splits the feature error into a \emph{scale mismatch} $\Delta\rho$ (activation-magnitude collapse) and a \emph{shape mismatch} $1-\Omega$ (feature-manifold distortion); a covariance-weighted head-discrepancy term closes the bound.
Each axis is independently measurable from a single forward pass, so a PRISM failure is not merely a flag but a \emph{direction}: scale-dominated failures localize the loss to outlier-channel corruption, shape-dominated to distorted feature geometry between tokens, and head-dominated to perturbation of the prediction head while the backbone is preserved.

\paragraph{From diagnostic to training signal.}
The same quantity that diagnoses a variant's drift can also prevent it.
Under frozen-head LoRA the head term vanishes and the risk gap is governed by backbone shape drift, which $\Omega$ captures differentiably.
Adding a weighted penalty on $1-\Omega$ to the fine-tuning objective converts the bound from a post-hoc diagnostic into an active constraint against catastrophic forgetting (Sec.~\ref{subsec:shape_reg_exp}): even a weak weight suffices to reduce downstream forgetting across every benchmark we study.

\paragraph{Contributions.}
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*,nosep,topsep=2pt]
    \item \textbf{Theory: a closed-form CE risk bound with three diagnostic axes, from an exact scale--shape decomposition.} The Procrustes feature-alignment residual splits exactly into scale $(\Delta\rho)^2$ and shape $2\rho_T\rho_P(1{-}\Omega)$ arms; combined with a covariance-weighted head-discrepancy term, this yields a closed-form upper bound on the cross-entropy risk gap $|\mathcal{R}_T - \mathcal{R}_P|$ (Theorem~\ref{thm:unified_bound}).

    \item \textbf{Framework: a unified, label-free diagnostic that doubles as a training objective.} The bound is computed entirely from forward-pass features and instantiates cleanly across both PTQ and frozen-head LoRA; via the differentiable trace form $\Omega$, it directly becomes a training-time shape regularizer against catastrophic forgetting (Sec.~\ref{subsec:action}).

    \item \textbf{Empirical: rank-consistent across both regimes, with axis-level localization.} Across the cross-family Llama--Qwen pair (with Ministral and DeepSeek replications in Appendix~\ref{app:per_model_tables}) over five benchmarks, the bound ranks variants with mean Spearman $|r_s|{\approx}0.82$ on PTQ and $0.835$ on LoRA forgetting; its three axes localize distinct empirical regimes (shape distortion at Q2/Q3 PTQ, head divergence at Qwen3 Q6\_K/Q8\_0, scale-axis separability under cross-task LoRA drift), and a mild shape regularizer reduces forgetting on every downstream benchmark.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Related Work}

\paragraph{Representational Similarity and Procrustes Shape Metrics.}
Representational similarity statistics---SVCCA~\cite{raghu2017svcca}, CKA~\cite{kornblith2019similarity}, and the generalized shape metrics framework~\cite{williams2021generalized}---measure geometric resemblance between activation matrices, with the latter's squared Procrustes distance already carrying the nuclear norm $\|Z_T^\top Z_P\|_*$ that appears in our $\Omega_N$. The link from these summaries to downstream behavior remains an open problem~\cite{ding2021grounding, davari2023reliability, klabunde2023similarity}; a recent decodability bound~\cite{harvey2024what} reaches downstream via whitened kernels and newly-trained linear probes, a different object than the deployed prediction heads we evaluate. The Linear Representation Hypothesis~\cite{moschella2022relative, park2023linear} motivates restricting alignment to the orthogonal group, and the Platonic Representation Hypothesis~\cite{huh2024platonic} corroborates same-lineage shared geometry. Our formulation differs in retaining activation scale via $(\Delta\rho)^2$, modeling head mismatch through a covariance-weighted term, and lifting the Procrustes residual into a closed-form upper bound on cross-entropy risk evaluated on each model's deployed head.

\paragraph{Post-Training Quantization and Its Evaluation.}
Post-training quantization is the dominant deployment path for LLMs under memory constraints: GPTQ~\cite{frantar2022gptq} uses approximate second-order information for layer-wise reconstruction; GGUF and BitsAndBytes~\cite{dettmers2022gpt3} are simpler round-to-nearest schemes whose degradation tracks activation outliers~\cite{xiao2023smoothquant}. Existing cheap diagnostics---layer-wise reconstruction loss or weight quantization error---are indirect proxies that do not account for nonlinear error accumulation through the full network. PRISM contributes a complementary, generation-free geometric decomposition: scale collapse, structural distortion, and head divergence are each measured from a single forward pass, localizing which axis carries the degradation.

\paragraph{Catastrophic Forgetting under Parameter-Efficient Fine-Tuning.}
LoRA-based fine-tuning~\cite{hu2022lora} risks catastrophic forgetting of pre-training knowledge, even when the \texttt{lm\_head} stays frozen. Weight-space remedies---EWC~\cite{kirkpatrick2017overcoming} and replay-based methods---constrain parameter movement; a recent geometric analysis~\cite{steele2026subspace} empirically fits LoRA forgetting to \emph{gradient}-subspace angles. Our formulation uses \emph{hidden-state} geometry: feature-manifold drift ($\Omega$, $\Delta\rho$) relative to the frozen base bounds the downstream risk gap and is usable both as a post-hoc diagnostic and as a training-time penalty (Sec.~\ref{subsec:shape_reg}).

\paragraph{Efficient Evaluation and Performance Forecasting.}
Scaling laws~\cite{kaplan2020scaling, hoffmann2022training} predict aggregate loss but not variant-specific degradation; item-response-theory subsets~\cite{polo2024tinybenchmarks, perlitz2024efficient} preserve variant orderings without linking representational change to expected accuracy shift; weak-to-strong generalization~\cite{burns2023weak} targets label generation rather than risk estimation. PRISM is geometry-grounded: the bound is computed directly from representational geometry and decomposes each variant's degradation into three diagnostic axes.

\section{A Geometric Framework for Proxy Performance Estimation}
\label{sec:geometric_foundation}

We derive a closed-form upper bound on the cross-entropy risk gap $|\mathcal{R}_T - \mathcal{R}_P|$ between a target model and a proxy, grounded entirely in their feature geometry. We first state the setup, then prove an exact decomposition of the bound into three diagnostic axes (scale, shape, head), explain the identity-alignment form ($\Omega$ at $W{=}I$) used throughout our experiments, extend the bound to autoregressive generation, and finally lift it into a training-time regularizer.

\subsection{Setup}
\label{subsec:problem_setup}

We consider a \emph{Target model} $T$ (e.g., a full-precision base) and a \emph{Proxy model} $P$ (e.g., a quantized or fine-tuned variant) with shared hidden dimension $d$. Each model factors into a Transformer backbone $\phi_M: \mathcal{X} \to \mathbb{R}^d$ followed by a linear prediction head $H_M \in \mathbb{R}^{d \times V}$ (the \texttt{lm\_head} projecting onto a vocabulary of size $V$):
\begin{equation}
f_M(x) = \mathrm{softmax}\!\big(\phi_M(x) \cdot H_M\big), \qquad M \in \{T, P\}.
\end{equation}
The risk of $M$ under data distribution $\mathcal{D}$ is the cross-entropy expectation
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:ce_def}
\mathcal{R}_M \;=\; \mathbb{E}_{(x,y) \sim \mathcal{D}}\!\big[\ell\big(\phi_M(x) H_M,\, y\big)\big], \qquad \ell(v, y) = -v_y + \log \textstyle\sum_{j} e^{v_j}, \; v \in \mathbb{R}^V.
\end{equation}
Following the \emph{Linear Representation Hypothesis}~\cite{moschella2022relative, park2023linear}, we assume the two backbones lie in a common lineage: there exists an orthogonal $W \in \mathcal{O}(d)$ such that $\phi_T(x) \approx \phi_P(x)\, W$ for all $x$. On a calibration sample of size $n$, we collect features into $Z_T, Z_P \in \mathbb{R}^{n \times d}$, denote the columns of $H_M$ by $h_{M,1},\ldots,h_{M,V}$, and define the RMS feature scale $\rho_M = \|Z_M\|_F / \sqrt{n}$ and the empirical second-moment matrix $\Sigma_P = Z_P^\top Z_P / n$.


\subsection{The Unified Risk Bound}
\label{subsec:unified_bound}

To bound $|\mathcal{R}_T - \mathcal{R}_P|$ we introduce a hybrid risk $\mathcal{R}_{P \to T} := \mathbb{E}[\ell(\phi_P(x)\,W \cdot H_T, y)]$ that evaluates the target's head on the proxy's aligned features. The triangle inequality splits the gap into a \emph{feature error} $\delta := |\mathcal{R}_T - \mathcal{R}_{P\to T}|$ and a \emph{head error} $\gamma := |\mathcal{R}_{P\to T} - \mathcal{R}_P|$; each admits a closed-form bound whose full derivation is in Appendix~\ref{app:detailed_proofs}.

\paragraph{Feature error $\delta$.}
The cross-entropy loss is Lipschitz in the feature vector with constant $K_{\mathrm{feat}}$, derived via a simplex polarization argument in Appendix~\ref{app:kfeat}. The alignment residual $\|Z_T - Z_P W\|_F^2 / n$ admits an exact identity for every orthogonal $W$:

\begin{proposition}[Exact Scale--Shape Decomposition]
\label{prop:exact_decomposition}
For any $W \in \mathcal{O}(d)$,
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:exact_equality}
\frac{1}{n} \| Z_T - Z_P W \|_F^2
\;=\;
\underbrace{(\rho_T - \rho_P)^2}_{\text{\rm Scale Mismatch } (\Delta\rho)^2}
\;+\;
\underbrace{2 \rho_T \rho_P \big( 1 - \Omega_W \big)}_{\text{\rm Shape Mismatch}},
\end{equation}
where $\Omega_W$ is the \emph{trace Procrustes similarity}:
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:omega_def}
\Omega_W(Z_T, Z_P) \;:=\; \frac{\operatorname{Tr}(Z_T^\top Z_P\, W)}{\| Z_T \|_F\, \| Z_P \|_F} \;\in [-1,\, 1].
\end{equation}
\end{proposition}

Combined with the Lipschitz bound (Appendix~\ref{app:detailed_proofs}):
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:delta_bound}
\delta \;\le\; K_{\mathrm{feat}} \sqrt{(\rho_T - \rho_P)^2 + 2\rho_T \rho_P (1 - \Omega_W)}.
\end{equation}

\paragraph{Head error $\gamma$.}
Let $\Delta H := W H_T - H_P$ for the alignment $W$; then $\gamma \le K_{\mathrm{pred}}\,\|\Sigma_P^{1/2}\Delta H\|_F$ with $K_{\mathrm{pred}} \le \sqrt{2}$ (Appendix~\ref{app:head_bound}). The covariance weighting $\Sigma_P^{1/2}$ ensures that only head misalignment within the \emph{active subspace} of the data contributes.

\begin{theorem}[Unified Risk Bound]
\label{thm:unified_bound}
For any $W \in \mathcal{O}(d)$,
\begin{equation}
|\mathcal{R}_T - \mathcal{R}_P| \;\le\; \underbrace{K_{\mathrm{feat}} \sqrt{ (\rho_T - \rho_P)^2 + 2\rho_T \rho_P \big(1 - \Omega_W\big) }}_{\text{\rm Geometric Alignment Error}} \;+\; \underbrace{K_{\mathrm{pred}} \,\| \Sigma_P^{1/2} (W H_T - H_P) \|_F}_{\text{\rm Second-Moment-Weighted Head Error}}.
\end{equation}
\end{theorem}


\subsection{Three Diagnostic Axes}
\label{subsec:interpretation}

Theorem~\ref{thm:unified_bound} decomposes the risk gap along three \emph{independently measurable} axes, each attached to a distinct failure mode of the proxy.

\paragraph{Scale mismatch $\Delta\rho$.}
Divergence in activation magnitude directly amplifies the feature error. Aggressive bit-width reduction clips activation outliers~\cite{dettmers2022gpt3, xiao2023smoothquant}, shrinking $\rho_P < \rho_T$; the scale axis isolates this \emph{scale collapse}.

\paragraph{Shape mismatch $1-\Omega_W$.}
The shape term captures the distortion of the feature manifold up to rotation. A drop in $\Omega_W$ signals that the relative arrangement of token representations has been corrupted---what we term \emph{structural distortion}.

\paragraph{Head divergence $\|\Sigma_P^{1/2}(W H_T - H_P)\|_F$.}
The head term quantifies how differently the two prediction heads interpret features, weighted by $\Sigma_P$ so that only directions where the data has support can contribute.

\paragraph{PRISM admits a family of Procrustes similarities.}
The family $\{\Omega_W\}$ of Prop.~\ref{prop:exact_decomposition} includes the trace form $\Omega_{W=I}$ and the nuclear form $\Omega_N := \Omega_{W=W_N}$ (Appendix~\ref{app:tightness}); a related Frobenius similarity $\Omega_F$, tied to CKA, is discussed there as an external comparison. The main text commits to $\Omega := \Omega_{W=I}$ because the frozen-head regimes studied here---LoRA and FP16-head PTQ---keep $H_T = H_P$ in most configurations, so the head term simplifies at $W = I$ and leaves the risk gap clean to analyze; the $W = W_N$ counterpart is reported as a Sec.~\ref{subsec:ablation} ablation.


\subsection{Extension to Autoregressive Generation}
\label{subsec:ar_extension}

The bound applies unchanged to sequence-level generation. For a sequence $(c, y)$ with context $c$ and target continuation $y = (y_1, \ldots, y_{|y|})$, the autoregressive risk is
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:ar_risk}
\mathcal{R}_M^{\mathrm{AR}} \;=\; \mathbb{E}_{(c, y) \sim \mathcal{D}} \!\left[ \frac{1}{|y|} \sum_{\tau=1}^{|y|} \ell\!\big(\phi_M(c, y_{<\tau}) \cdot H_M,\; y_\tau\big) \right].
\end{equation}
Under teacher forcing, all token-level features $\phi_M(c, y_{<\tau})$ are extracted in a single forward pass and collected into a matrix $Z_M^{\mathrm{AR}} \in \mathbb{R}^{N \times d}$ (with $N$ the total number of target tokens across the calibration set); Theorem~\ref{thm:unified_bound} then applies directly to $(Z_T^{\mathrm{AR}}, Z_P^{\mathrm{AR}})$, unifying point-wise classification, language modeling, short-horizon QA, and multi-step reasoning under a single bound. The full corollary is in Appendix~\ref{app:ar_extension}.


\subsection{From Diagnostic to Training: Shape Regularization}
\label{subsec:shape_reg}

LoRA fine-tuning drives the backbone shape away from its base: $\Omega(Z_0, Z_t)$ drops as training proceeds, and---because the head is frozen---the full risk gap between base $\theta_0$ and checkpoint $\theta_t$ reduces to backbone drift alone,
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:lora_bound}
|\mathcal{R}_0 - \mathcal{R}_t| \;\le\; K_{\mathrm{feat}}\sqrt{(\rho_0-\rho_t)^2 + 2\rho_0\rho_t(1-\Omega)}.
\end{equation}
Constraining shape should therefore stabilize target-task learning and suppress catastrophic forgetting on downstream tasks at once. Since $1 - \Omega$ is differentiable in $Z_t$, we augment the fine-tuning objective with a shape-preserving penalty:
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:shape_reg}
L_{\mathrm{total}} \;=\; \underbrace{L_{\mathrm{CE}}\bigl(\theta_t;\,\mathcal{D}_{\mathrm{FT}}\bigr)}_{\text{task loss}} \;+\; \lambda\,\bigl(1 - \Omega\!\bigl(Z_0^{\text{ref}},\, Z_t^{\text{ref}}\bigr)\bigr),
\end{equation}
where $Z_0^{\text{ref}}, Z_t^{\text{ref}}$ are base and current feature matrices on a fixed reference set (training schedule and overhead in Sec.~\ref{subsec:exp_setup}); empirical effect is validated in Sec.~\ref{subsec:shape_reg_exp}.


\section{Applications Across the LLM Post-Training Lifecycle}
\label{sec:applications}

The Unified Risk Bound (Theorem~\ref{thm:unified_bound}) instantiates in two LLM lifecycle settings---post-training quantization and frozen-head LoRA fine-tuning---which differ in which term of the bound dominates the risk gap.

\paragraph{Quantization quality estimation.}
\label{subsec:quantization}
Target $T$ is the BF16 base model and proxy $P$ is the quantized variant. PTQ perturbs each weight independently \emph{without applying any basis transformation}, so $W{=}I$ is the natural alignment and the proxy's features stay comparable to the target's coordinate-by-coordinate. When the protocol keeps \texttt{lm\_head} in FP16 (GPTQ, BitsAndBytes), the head term $\gamma$ vanishes and the bound reduces to the feature side $(\Delta\rho)^2$ and $1{-}\Omega$; GGUF k-quant tiers that also quantize the output embedding contribute a non-negligible $\gamma$.

\paragraph{Geometric monitoring of catastrophic forgetting.}
\label{subsec:forgetting}
Target $T{=}\theta_0$ is the frozen base model and proxy $P{=}\theta_t$ is the fine-tuned checkpoint, both evaluated on downstream benchmarks \emph{distinct} from the fine-tuning task so that $|\mathcal{R}_T - \mathcal{R}_P|$ captures catastrophic forgetting. Under standard LoRA~\cite{hu2022lora} the \texttt{lm\_head} stays frozen, so $\gamma$ vanishes and forgetting is governed entirely by \emph{backbone geometric drift} $(\Delta\rho, 1{-}\Omega)$; the shape regularizer of Sec.~\ref{subsec:shape_reg} directly penalizes the shape term $1{-}\Omega$.

\section{Experiments}
\label{sec:experiments}

We organize the experiments around three claims tied to the three contributions: \emph{Predictiveness}---the bound tracks $|\Delta\mathcal{R}|$ in rank order across both PTQ and LoRA variants (Sec.~\ref{subsec:predict}); \emph{Decomposability}---its three axes localize three qualitatively distinct empirical failure modes (Sec.~\ref{subsec:decompose}); and \emph{Actionability}---the differentiable shape arm doubles as a training-time regularizer that suppresses catastrophic forgetting (Sec.~\ref{subsec:action}). Sec.~\ref{subsec:ablation} closes with a component-wise ablation. Throughout, rank consistency is measured by Spearman $|r_s|$ between the bound and the empirical cross-entropy risk gap.

\subsection{Experimental Setup}
\label{subsec:exp_setup}

\paragraph{Models.}
The main analysis uses the cross-family pair Llama-3.1-8B~\cite{grattafiori2024llama} and Qwen3-8B~\cite{qwen2025qwen3}---differing in pre-training corpus, depth, vocabulary, and tokenizer family---for both quantization and LoRA experiments. PTQ replications on Ministral-3-8B and DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Llama-8B and on the instruction-tuned counterparts of Llama, Ministral, and Qwen are in Appendix~\ref{app:per_model_tables}; the Qwen LoRA replication is in Appendix~\ref{app:qwen_forgetting}.

\paragraph{Quantization Protocols.}
Three PTQ families across bit-widths 2--8, spanning basic rounding to calibration-based compensation: \textbf{GGUF} (round-to-nearest, \texttt{Q2\_K}--\texttt{Q8\_0}), \textbf{GPTQ}~\cite{frantar2022gptq} (4-bit, second-order reconstruction), and \textbf{BitsAndBytes}~\cite{dettmers2022gpt3} (INT8, NF4, FP4).

\paragraph{Fine-Tuning Tasks.}
LoRA fine-tunes on \textbf{TruthfulQA} (factual grounding) and \textbf{BBQ} (Bias Benchmark for QA, social-context reasoning)---two tasks chosen to elicit different drift geometries (Sec.~\ref{subsec:decompose}). Downstream forgetting is measured on the five standard knowledge and reasoning benchmarks below, all disjoint from either fine-tuning task.

\paragraph{Benchmarks and Scoring.}
Following Eq.~(\ref{eq:ar_risk}), five benchmarks: \textbf{MMLU}, \textbf{ARC} (multiple-choice knowledge), \textbf{TriviaQA}, \textbf{SQuAD} (short-horizon QA), and \textbf{GSM8K} (multi-step reasoning); language modeling (WikiText, FineWeb-Edu) is in Appendix~\ref{app:per_model_tables}. All risks are computed teacher-forced---prompt $c$ and targets $y$ scored in a single forward pass over the gold span---matching each benchmark's scoring rule by construction and producing deterministic per-sample residuals on the same scale as the LoRA fine-tuning objective. PRISM is a variant-ranking signal complementary to generation-based evaluation, not a substitute.

\paragraph{Calibration and Hyperparameters.}
PRISM and $|\Delta\mathcal{R}|$ are evaluated on fixed held-out subsets shared across all variants of a base ($N{=}512$ per benchmark for PTQ, $N{=}256$ for LoRA forgetting; TruthfulQA and BBQ use their full test sets). Because every variant sees the same subset, selection variance enters $|\Delta\mathcal{R}|$ and the bound identically and cannot affect their ranking; the orderings themselves span 1--2 orders of magnitude in $|\Delta\mathcal{R}|$ (e.g., Q2\_K vs.\ Q8\_0). For shape regularization (Sec.~\ref{subsec:action}) we sweep $\lambda \in \{0.0, 0.1, 0.5\}$; $\Omega$ is computed on a fixed batch of $32$ sequences from the pre-training distribution (disjoint from $\mathcal{D}_{\mathrm{FT}}$), refreshed every $k{=}8$ micro-steps. Checkpoints every $25$ steps, analysis at step $300$.


\subsection{Predictiveness: The Bound Tracks the Risk Gap}
\label{subsec:predict}

\paragraph{Quantization.}
Fig.~\ref{fig:quant_grid_bound} demonstrates PTQ predictiveness: the PRISM bound $\mathcal{B}$ tracks $|\Delta\mathcal{R}|$ with strong rank correlation across the cross-family Llama--Qwen pair (mean $|r_s|{\approx}0.82$ across the $2{\times}5$ grid; the same pattern replicates on Ministral and DeepSeek in Appendix Fig.~\ref{fig:quant_grid_bound_extra}). Two patterns hold consistently: (i)~\emph{bit-width drives the bound monotonically}---Q8/Q6 GGUF variants sit in the low-$\mathcal{B}$/low-$|\Delta\mathcal{R}|$ corner, while Q2 configurations move into the upper-right region where shape distortion $(1{-}\Omega)$ dominates the feature error; (ii)~\emph{family ordering is preserved across benchmarks}---BitsAndBytes NF4/FP4 and GGUF Q3 cluster, while GPTQ-4bit (Llama, DeepSeek) sits consistently lower in $\mathcal{B}$, reflecting its second-order Hessian compensation. \paragraph{LoRA forgetting.}
The same predictiveness extends to fine-tuning-induced drift (Fig.~\ref{fig:forget_grid_llama}): as LoRA fine-tuning proceeds on TruthfulQA or BBQ, the bound computed on \emph{downstream} benchmarks tracks the empirical cross-entropy drift step-by-step, with mean Spearman $|r_s|{=}0.835$ aggregated over both fine-tuning tasks and the five downstream benchmarks---comparable to the $0.82$ obtained on the PTQ grid (Fig.~\ref{fig:quant_grid_bound}). Because the head is frozen, $\gamma{=}0$ and $\mathcal{B}$ reduces to backbone scale and shape drift. Crucially, the correlation holds \emph{benchmark-by-benchmark}---each of ARC, MMLU, SQuAD, TriviaQA, GSM8K (including GSM8K, whose distribution differs substantially from the fine-tuning data)---consistent with backbone geometry being the locus of the forgetting signal across distinct evaluation families. The Qwen3-8B replication (Appendix~\ref{app:qwen_forgetting}) reproduces both patterns. Together, the PTQ and LoRA results show that a single closed-form bound transfers across two qualitatively different sources of model variation.


\subsection{Decomposability: Three Axes Localize Three Failure Modes}
\label{subsec:decompose}

A scalar correlation alone would already match prior work on representational similarity. The decomposition contributes the next layer: the dominant axis of Theorem~\ref{thm:unified_bound} reads off directly from the per-variant numbers and corresponds to a distinct empirical failure mode. Three regimes recur in our experiments.

\paragraph{Shape distortion (low-bit PTQ).}
At Q2 and Q3 across all four lineages tested (Appendix~\ref{app:per_model_tables} for Ministral and DeepSeek), the shape term $2\rho_T\rho_P(1{-}\Omega)$ outweighs the scale term $(\Delta\rho)^2$ by two to three orders of magnitude (e.g., Llama-Q2\_K on MMLU: $24$ vs.\ $\sim$$9{,}000$; Ministral-Q2\_K on MMLU: $\sim$$9$ vs.\ $\sim$$11{,}000$)---consistent with low-bit PTQ corrupting the relational structure of the feature manifold rather than its global scale. Scale drift is measurable across the PTQ grid but does not cleanly dominate any single variant; the most scale-affected case is Qwen3-Base Q2\_K on GSM8K (Table~\ref{tab:qwen_decomposition_all}, Appendix~\ref{app:per_model_tables}), where $\rho_P$ rises from $267$ to $313$ alongside shape drift.

\paragraph{Head divergence (GGUF k-quant tiers that touch \texttt{lm\_head}).}
A second regime appears when the protocol quantizes the output embedding. At Qwen3-Base Q6\_K on MMLU (Table~\ref{tab:qwen_decomposition_all}, Appendix~\ref{app:per_model_tables}), backbone scale and shape are essentially perfect ($(\Delta\rho)^2{=}0.56$, $\Omega{=}1.0000$), so $\delta{=}0.75$, yet the quantized output embedding alone contributes $\gamma{=}60.16$---making $\gamma$ essentially the entire bound (Q8\_0 shows the same pattern: $\delta{=}0.15$, $\gamma{=}19.0$). Under BnB INT8 the same Qwen3-Base keeps $\gamma{\equiv}0$ by construction, leaving $\delta{=}13.2$ as the sole error source---a qualitatively different decomposition determined entirely by which protocol quantizes \texttt{lm\_head}. The decomposition makes this protocol-level switch read off directly, identifying not just the magnitude of degradation but its dominant channel.

\paragraph{Scale-axis separability (LoRA cross-task contrast).}
The forgetting experiments reveal a third regime hidden by scalar metrics. Under TruthfulQA fine-tuning (Fig.~\ref{fig:forget_grid_llama} top row) we observe large shape drift with modest scale change; under BBQ fine-tuning (bottom row) both axes move. The decomposition $(\Delta\rho, 1{-}\Omega)$ in Eq.~(\ref{eq:lora_bound}) thus exhibits the two arms as non-redundant channels: \emph{different source tasks induce qualitatively different drift geometries}, and the scale axis is empirically separable from shape---an observation a single-number similarity would mask.

\paragraph{From diagnosis to remediation.}
Each dominant axis suggests a different remediation---per-channel outlier smoothing~\cite{xiao2023smoothquant} for scale collapse, Hessian-aware reconstruction for shape distortion, and FP16-\texttt{lm\_head} retention for head divergence. The bound thus turns from a scalar score into an axis-level diagnostic with actionable structure. We turn the differentiable shape arm into a training-time regularizer in Sec.~\ref{subsec:action}.

The decomposition of Sec.~\ref{subsec:decompose} suggests an immediate intervention: since shape drift dominates LoRA forgetting and $1{-}\Omega$ is differentiable in $Z_t$, penalizing it during fine-tuning should preserve downstream task knowledge. Fig.~\ref{fig:shape_reg_combined_llama} and Table~\ref{tab:trace_norm_llama_truthfulqa} answer the question raised by Sec.~\ref{subsec:shape_reg}: \emph{does minimizing $(1{-}\Omega)$ during fine-tuning actually suppress forgetting?} For Llama-3.1-8B fine-tuned on TruthfulQA, Table~\ref{tab:trace_norm_llama_truthfulqa} reports the decomposition at step $300$: as $\lambda$ rises from $0.0$ to $0.5$, $\Omega$ on downstream benchmarks increases monotonically (e.g., ARC $0.82{\to}0.86{\to}0.93$, MMLU $0.87{\to}0.90{\to}0.95$, GSM8K $0.94{\to}0.97{\to}1.00$), and downstream $|\Delta\mathcal{R}|$ drops on every benchmark. Three patterns emerge from Fig.~\ref{fig:shape_reg_combined_llama}: (i)~\emph{the reduction is uniform across benchmarks}---ARC, MMLU, SQuAD, TriviaQA, GSM8K all move in the same direction under the same $\lambda$, suggesting the regularizer acts on a benchmark-agnostic backbone property rather than overfitting any single evaluation; (ii)~\emph{the trade-off is monotone and controllable}---increasing $\lambda$ strengthens downstream preservation predictably, the behavior a bound-motivated regularizer should exhibit; (iii)~\emph{even the smallest setting $\lambda{=}0.1$ already eliminates a substantial fraction of forgetting}, indicating that a weak geometric prior is enough to curb backbone drift. The Qwen3-8B replication and BBQ per-variant tables are in Appendix~\ref{app:qwen_forgetting}.


\subsection{Ablation: Component-Wise Contribution to Variant Ranking}
\label{subsec:ablation}

\input{tables/quantization/baseline_combined}

Table~\ref{tab:baseline_combined} measures the marginal ranking contribution of each component of the PRISM bound by progressively adding them. Under the identity-alignment form ($W{=}I$, top block), the shape-only similarity $\Omega$ already achieves mean $|r_s|{=}0.80$, indicating that the relational geometry of token features alone carries most of the variant-ranking signal. Adding the scale term to form the feature alignment error $\delta$ lifts the mean by $+0.064$ to $0.87$ and wins the plurality of cells, making scale collapse the single largest per-component contributor. Adding the covariance-weighted head term to form the full bound $\mathcal{B}$ brings the aggregate mean slightly down to $0.82$: because our suite mixes protocols that quantize the output embedding ($\gamma{>}0$ for GGUF k-quant tiers) with protocols that keep it in FP16 ($\gamma{\equiv}0$ for GPTQ and BnB), the $\gamma$ term injects non-monotone variance across the pooled scatter. Two observations keep $\mathcal{B}$ the right \emph{default} metric. First, it is the certified upper bound of Theorem~\ref{thm:unified_bound} and is strictly required whenever $\gamma{>}0$; $\delta$ alone is not a valid bound in that regime. Second, $\mathcal{B}$ has the smallest Llama--Qwen gap ($\approx 0.015$ vs.\ $0.026$ and $0.042$ for $\delta$ and $\Omega$), so its ranking performance is the most robust across the two lineages tested.

Under the Procrustes-optimal alignment $W{=}W_N$ (bottom block), the cumulative pattern reverses cleanly: $\Omega_N \to \delta_N \to \mathcal{B}_N$ all increase monotonically, and the full bound $\mathcal{B}_N$ attains the strongest aggregate ranking ($|r_s|{=}0.91$, $8/10$ cells)---consistent with the head term being beneficial \emph{when the alignment can accommodate the non-identity rotations} between $H_T$ and $H_P$, and with the non-monotonicity observed under $W{=}I$ tracing to the alignment rather than the bound design itself. The two blocks together support Theorem~\ref{thm:unified_bound} at $W_N$ and quantify the trade-off at $W{=}I$: the trace specialization sacrifices $\sim$$0.09$ Spearman for autograd compatibility (no SVD per step), the head-term simplification under frozen \texttt{lm\_head}, and consistency with the regularizer of Sec.~\ref{subsec:shape_reg}. We continue to recommend $\mathcal{B}$ as our default and $\mathcal{B}_N$ as a post-hoc reference when the additional SVD cost is acceptable.

\section{Conclusion and Future Work}
\label{sec:conclusion}

We presented PRISM, a geometric framework with three contributions. \textbf{Theory}: the Orthogonal Procrustes residual splits exactly into scale $(\Delta\rho)^2$ and shape $2\rho_T\rho_P(1{-}\Omega)$ arms; combined with a covariance-weighted head term, this yields a closed-form upper bound on the cross-entropy risk gap $|\mathcal{R}_T - \mathcal{R}_P|$ along three independently measurable axes (Theorem~\ref{thm:unified_bound}). \textbf{Framework}: the bound is label-free and instantiates across both PTQ and frozen-head LoRA; via the differentiable trace form $\Omega$, it doubles as a training-time regularizer against catastrophic forgetting. \textbf{Empirical}: rank-consistent across both regimes (mean Spearman $|r_s|{\approx}0.82$ on PTQ, $0.835$ on LoRA forgetting), the three axes localize distinct empirical regimes---shape distortion at Q2/Q3 PTQ, head divergence at Qwen3 Q6\_K/Q8\_0, scale-axis separability under cross-task LoRA drift---and the shape regularizer reduces forgetting on every downstream benchmark.

\paragraph{Scope and limitations.}
PRISM is a \emph{relative} metric: it scores how a variant differs from a reference base via the cross-entropy risk gap, not the variant's absolute task quality---the strong rank correlation reported in Sec.~\ref{subsec:predict} supports variant comparison but does not predict $|\Delta\mathcal{R}|$ itself. The shape regularizer of Sec.~\ref{subsec:action} provides one direct training-time intervention against the shape axis; protocol-level mitigations for the scale and head axes (per-channel outlier smoothing, Hessian-aware reconstruction, FP16-\texttt{lm\_head} retention) are themselves a focused empirical follow-up the diagnostic enables.

\paragraph{Future Work.}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*,nosep,topsep=2pt]
    \item \textbf{Beyond LoRA forgetting.} Extending the PRISM shape regularizer to full SFT and distillation---where backbone drift is substantially larger---would test the limits of shape anchoring as an active intervention.
    \item \textbf{Diagnostic applications.} The PRISM bound is computed entirely from forward-pass features and is therefore label-free, enabling three near-term uses: \emph{per-sample OOD detection} via scale and shape residuals on individual inputs; \emph{cross-scale hyperparameter transfer} using the bound between a small proxy and its scaled target to predict tuning robustness; and \emph{production drift monitoring} on live traffic, where no labels are required.
    \item \textbf{Beyond LLMs.} Vision Transformers and contrastive image encoders such as SigLIP share the same backbone-then-linear-head structure as LLMs, and final-layer LayerNorm enforces a tight feature-scale distribution---making the PRISM scale axis naturally small and the bound primarily a shape-and-head diagnostic. ViT quantization, vision-encoder distillation, and cross-modal alignment (consistent with the Platonic Representation Hypothesis~\cite{huh2024platonic}) are natural targets.
\end{itemize}
