\caption{\textbf{PRISM decomposition of the risk gap.} The cross-entropy risk gap $|\mathcal{R}_T{-}\mathcal{R}_P|$ between target $T$ and proxy $P$ is bounded by a feature alignment error $\delta$ and a head discrepancy $\gamma$ (Thm.~\ref{thm:unified_bound}). Under Procrustes alignment $W^{*}{=}\arg\min_{W\in\mathcal{O}(d)}\|Z_T{-}Z_PW\|_F$, $\delta$ decomposes exactly into \emph{scale mismatch} $(\Delta\rho)^{2}{=}(\rho_T{-}\rho_P)^{2}$ and \emph{shape mismatch} $2\rho_T\rho_P(1{-}\Omega)$ (Prop.~\ref{prop:exact_decomposition}); $\gamma$ measures $\Delta H{=}W^{*}H_T{-}H_P$ re-weighted by the proxy covariance $\Sigma_P$, vanishing when $H_T{=}H_P$ (frozen-head LoRA).}
